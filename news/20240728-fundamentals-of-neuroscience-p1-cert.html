Certificate Achievement in Neuroscience

July 28, 2024

I'm thrilled to share that I've earned a certificate for completing "Fundamentals of Neuroscience, Part 1: Electrical Properties of Neurons" on edX. This course, offered by Harvard University, provided me with an in-depth understanding of bioelectricity, the importance of resting potential, passive membrane properties, action potentials, and their propagation in the nervous system. Through interactive simulations and DIY labs, I learned how individual neurons use electricity to transmit information, building a foundation for comprehending the entire nervous system's function. Course Overview

Here's a brief overview of what the course covered:

  • Fundamentals of Bioelectricity
    • Basics of bioelectric signals
    • Understanding membrane potentials
    • Action potentials and their properties
  • How You Can Do Neuroscience in Your Home
    • Simple neuroscience experiments
    • DIY neuroscience tools and techniques
    • Ethical considerations in home neuroscience experiments
  • How Neurons Communicate with Each Other
    • Synaptic transmission mechanisms
    • Chemical vs. electrical synapses
    • Neurotransmitters and their receptors
  • How Sensory Perception Works in the Brain
    • Sensory pathways and processing
    • Visual, auditory, and somatosensory systems
    • Integration of sensory information
  • The Basic Anatomy of Functional Areas of the Brain
    • Overview of major brain regions
    • Functional areas and their roles
    • Brain plasticity and functional reorganization
